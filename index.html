Michael is a founder, speaker, and investor known for building and scaling consumer technology and brand-driven businesses.

He is a General Partner at Science Inc., a Santa Monica-based venture studio that co-founds companies from day zero and helps founders turn early conviction into operating momentum—product, distribution, hiring, and go-to-market execution.

2011 - Present

Science Inc

In 2011, Jones founded Science Inc. to apply a studio approach to company creation: centralized operating capabilities, rapid testing, and repeatable growth systems designed to reduce the friction that slows most early-stage companies. Science has been involved with category-defining consumer brands and platforms, including Dollar Shave Club, Rover, and Liquid Death, among others.

2010

MySpace

Jones later became CEO of MySpace in 2010, stepping into one of the most visible turnaround roles in consumer internet. During his tenure he helped reposition the product and stabilize operations ahead of the company's sale to Specific Media. That combination—early-stage creation plus operational triage—became a defining thread of his career: building from scratch, then rebuilding when stakes are highest.

:)
2006

Userplane

Before Science, Jones helped shape early waves of social and digital media. He founded Userplane, an instant-messaging and embedded communications platform used by major consumer websites; Userplane was acquired by AOL in 2006. After AOL, he launched Tsavo Media, an online media network, which was acquired by Cyberplex for $75 million.

Mike is a frequent public speaker on founder psychology, the human traits behind outsized outcomes, and how modern tools—including AI—are changing the way people build companies, make decisions, and reinvent themselves.

Most Requested Topics

Topic One

Can AI help us become the architects of our own selves?

A sharp, personal talk about what happens when you use AI not as advice, but as a high-bandwidth mirror for behavior and decision-making. Michael shares the system he built to map traits, patterns, and blindspots across his own life—and how that model is now being turned into a platform called Human Architecture.

Audience leaves with

A usable model for “AI as reflection,” prompts that reveal patterns, and a way to turn insight into action without outsourcing agency.

Topic Two

Building impossible things with difficult people

Breakout outcomes often come from high-agency, intense, sometimes polarizing personalities. Michael unpacks why the traits that make someone hard to work with are often the same ones that make them capable of building something extraordinary—and how to tell the difference between signal and chaos.

Audience leaves with

A lens for evaluating temperament, early warning signals, and operating tactics for high-stakes, high-tension founding teams.

Topic Three

Impact of Social Media

A clear-eyed look at how social platforms shape attention, identity, incentives, and culture—from someone who has built, run, and invested across every era of social media.

  • How attention markets work: incentives, virality, outrage, and the “algorithm as environment.”
  • Second-order effects: mental health, loneliness, polarization, and the new status economy.
  • What brands and founders can learn: distribution primitives, community loops, and trust in the feed era.
  • Practical playbook: boundaries, detox design, and rebuilding agency (personal + organizational).
